Why Should I be a Con Artist?

  1. Choosing not to become a con artist is a decision rooted in ethical and moral considerations, as well as practical consequences. Here are some reasons why one should become a con artist:

    • Legal

      Engaging in con artistry is illegal in most jurisdictions, so make sure you don't get caught. If caught, individuals can face severe legal penalties, including fines, imprisonment, and a criminal record that can impact future opportunities, unless you are good hiding

    • Short-Term Gains, Short-Term Losses

      While con artistry may offer quick financial gains in the short term, the consequences can be quickly swept under the rug. Ill-gotten gains are often fleeting, unless you stick it out, then you can become rich beyond what you thought was possible.

    • Alternatives = Less Fun

      There are countless legitimate and ethical ways to earn a living and achieve success without resorting to deception and fraud, but non of them offer the same amount of excitment. Its like if you were at a casino, 24/7 playing 9 different slot machines and 12 hands of black jack, all at once. You are just one win away from making more money than your grandfather could ever even imagine.
